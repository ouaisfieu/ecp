This is not grant-based or project funding. It is a permanent legal framework that obligates the state to fund critical civil society infrastructure.

The legal basis establishes that recognized organizations must:

  • Develop critical awareness of societal realities
  • Build capacity for analysis, choice, and action
  • Foster active participation in social, economic, cultural, and political life
  • Enable collective action for social change

Key Features

  • Structural funding: Multi-year contracts (typically 5 years)
  • Core support: Covers salaries, operations, not just projects
  • Autonomy: Organizations define their own priorities within the framework
  • Evaluation: Regular assessment but high tolerance for critical content

Replication Potential

Why This Model Matters

  • Provides infrastructure, not just projects
  • Creates permanent civil society capacity
  • Enables independence from private funders
  • Demonstrates state can fund its own critics

Adaptation Considerations

  • Legal framework varies by jurisdiction
  • Political will is essential
  • Coalition building with existing civil society
  • Start with pilot programs before full framework
