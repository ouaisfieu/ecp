The core idea: Create a permanent legal framework that obligates the state to fund civil society organizations whose mission is to develop citizens' critical capacity and collective action for social change.

The 6-Step Replication Process

Research Existing Frameworks

Map current funding mechanisms in your country. What laws exist for civil society funding? What are the gaps? Who are the allies in government and civil society?

Build a Coalition

Identify organizations that would benefit from structural funding. Bring together diverse actors: unions, feminist groups, environmental orgs, cultural associations. Collective voice = political power.

Draft the Legal Framework

Adapt the Belgian decree to your legal context. Key elements: clear mission definition, recognition criteria, funding categories, evaluation mechanisms, organizational autonomy.

Political Advocacy

Find political champions. Present the model as: democratic infrastructure, citizen empowerment, social cohesion. Frame it in terms that resonate with local political culture.

Pilot Program

Start small. Propose a pilot with 10-20 organizations. Demonstrate impact. Build evidence base for expansion.

Scale & Institutionalize

Once pilot proves successful, push for permanent legislation. Create oversight body. Establish sustainable funding mechanism.
